## Tailoring & Governance
Tailoring & Governance defines **how the SDLC can be adapted** to different project types, delivery methods, and organisational contexts while ensuring that governance, compliance, and quality standards are consistently applied.

---

### Objectives
The objectives of Tailoring & Governance are to:

* Provide flexibility in applying the SDLC to various delivery models (Waterfall, Agile, Product)
* Maintain consistency, traceability, and accountability across projects
* Enable proportionate governance based on project risk, complexity, and impact
* Ensure that teams understand mandatory controls versus adaptable practices
* Encourage continuous improvement and best practice adoption

---

### Key Principles
1. **Proportionality**

   * Governance and process requirements should scale to project size, risk, and complexity
   * High-risk systems require formal documentation, approvals, and audits; low-risk projects may use lighter-weight processes

2. **Mandatory Controls**

   * Some governance elements are non-negotiable (e.g., change approvals, traceability, audit logs, security checks)
   * These mandatory controls ensure organisational accountability and regulatory compliance

3. **Adaptable Practices**

   * Teams can tailor approaches for design, testing, deployment cadence, and documentation
   * Adaptations must maintain core principles of quality, traceability, and risk management

4. **Transparency**

   * Tailoring decisions must be documented and approved by relevant governance bodies
   * All stakeholders should understand what is tailored and why

5. **Continuous Improvement**

   * Tailoring should evolve based on lessons learned, metrics, and stakeholder feedback
   * Regular reviews ensure that the SDLC remains efficient, effective, and aligned with organisational objectives

---

### Key Activities
#### Define Tailoring Rules
* Identify which SDLC elements can be tailored for a specific project
* Specify thresholds, triggers, or conditions that determine governance requirements
* Document tailoring decisions, rationale, and approvals

#### Apply Governance Controls
* Ensure mandatory controls are applied consistently across all projects
* Integrate controls into tooling, pipelines, and approval processes
* Monitor adherence to mandatory governance requirements

#### Review and Authorise Tailoring
* Tailoring plans should be reviewed by project management, security, and governance stakeholders
* Approvals must be documented to maintain transparency and accountability

#### Continuous Monitoring and Adjustment
* Track the effectiveness of tailoring decisions using metrics and retrospectives
* Adjust governance and tailored practices based on lessons learned, emerging risks, or organisational changes

---

### Outputs and Deliverables
* Tailoring guidance and project-specific plans
* Documentation of mandatory vs adaptable processes
* Approval records and rationale for tailoring decisions
* Metrics demonstrating governance effectiveness
* Lessons learned and improvement recommendations

---

### Governance and Controls
Key governance principles:

* **Non-Negotiable Controls**: Certain processes, approvals, and traceability requirements must always be applied
* **Proportional Adaptation**: Tailoring must be justified, documented, and approved
* **Auditability**: Tailoring decisions and exceptions must be auditable
* **Visibility**: Stakeholders must be aware of the tailoring approach and its impact on processes
* **Accountability**: Owners are accountable for ensuring that tailored approaches meet organisational standards

**Control Outcome:**
Teams can **adapt the SDLC to project needs** while ensuring that essential governance, risk management, and compliance requirements are consistently maintained.

---

### Alignment Across Delivery Models
* **Agile / Product Models**:

  * Tailoring may focus on iterative planning, lighter documentation, and automated approvals
  * Governance is integrated into pipelines and feedback loops

* **Waterfall**:

  * Tailoring may adjust stage gate rigor, documentation templates, or review frequency
  * Core controls remain in place to support formal audit and compliance

---

### Anti-Patterns to Avoid
* Tailoring without approval or transparency
* Removing mandatory governance elements for convenience
* Overcomplicating processes beyond what is necessary for risk level
* Failing to document tailoring decisions or lessons learned
* Ignoring feedback loops and continuous improvement

---

### Closing Statement

Tailoring & Governance ensures that the SDLC is **flexible, efficient, and risk-aware**. Teams can adjust processes to project needs without compromising mandatory controls, traceability, or organisational accountability.
