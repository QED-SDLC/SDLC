## Risks & Dependencies
The Risks & Dependencies phase focuses on **identifying, assessing, monitoring, and mitigating risks** associated with software delivery, as well as tracking dependencies across systems, teams, and external parties. Proactive management ensures that potential issues are addressed early, and delivery remains predictable and resilient.

---

### Objectives
The objectives of Risks & Dependencies are to:

* Identify potential risks and dependencies early in the lifecycle
* Assess impact, likelihood, and priority of each risk or dependency
* Implement appropriate mitigation, contingency, or escalation strategies
* Provide visibility to stakeholders and governance bodies
* Reduce delivery delays, defects, and operational disruptions

---

### Key Activities
#### Risk Identification
* Identify risks at all levels: project, technical, operational, and organisational
* Consider risks arising from:

  * Technology or architecture
  * Integration or third-party dependencies
  * Regulatory or compliance requirements
  * Resource availability and skill gaps
  * External factors (vendors, market changes, infrastructure)

---

#### Risk Assessment and Prioritisation
* Evaluate each risk based on likelihood and potential impact
* Prioritise risks using a consistent framework (e.g., high/medium/low, or numeric scoring)
* Identify critical dependencies that could block delivery or compromise quality

---

#### Risk Mitigation and Management
* Develop mitigation plans for high-priority risks
* Assign ownership for monitoring and managing risks
* Implement preventive controls where possible
* Maintain contingency plans for unavoidable risks

---

#### Dependency Management
* Identify dependencies between teams, systems, and external vendors
* Track the status, readiness, and potential blockers of each dependency
* Communicate dependency requirements and schedules to all stakeholders
* Align dependency management with planning, release, and change processes

---

#### Monitoring and Reporting
* Continuously monitor risks and dependencies throughout the lifecycle
* Update risk registers and dependency logs with status, mitigation progress, and outcomes
* Escalate critical risks that cannot be mitigated at the team level
* Report risk and dependency status in metrics dashboards and governance forums

---

### Outputs and Deliverables
Key outputs include:

* Risk register and mitigation plans
* Dependency log and tracking artefacts
* Risk assessment reports for governance and audits
* Lessons learned from risk and dependency management
* Escalation records and decision logs

---

### Governance and Controls
Essential governance principles:

* **Ownership and Accountability**: Each risk and dependency must have a responsible owner
* **Proactive Identification**: Risks should be identified as early as possible
* **Escalation and Transparency**: Critical risks must be visible to leadership and governance bodies
* **Regular Review**: Risks and dependencies should be reviewed at defined intervals or triggered by events
* **Traceability**: Mitigations and decisions must be linked to risks and dependencies

**Control Outcome:**
Risks and dependencies are **visible, managed, and mitigated**, reducing surprises and ensuring smoother delivery.

---

### Alignment Across Delivery Models
* **Agile / Product Models**:

  * Risk and dependency management is continuous and iterative
  * Teams maintain a visible backlog of risks and blockers
  * Retrospectives provide a venue to review unresolved or emerging risks

* **Waterfall**:

  * Risks and dependencies are formally captured during planning and stage gates
  * Mitigation and contingency plans are documented in project plans
  * Periodic reviews ensure risk exposure is managed across phases

---

### Proportionality and Risk-Based Approach
* High-impact or high-complexity systems require more formal risk and dependency management, with documented mitigation plans and governance review
* Low-risk internal projects may manage risks informally, but ownership and visibility remain mandatory

---

### Anti-Patterns to Avoid
* Ignoring or delaying risk identification until problems occur
* Failing to assign clear ownership for risks or dependencies
* Managing risks only reactively rather than proactively
* Underestimating inter-team or external dependencies
* Treating risk registers as static documents rather than living artefacts

---

### Closing Statement

Proactive management of risks and dependencies ensures that potential problems are **identified early, mitigated effectively, and escalated appropriately**, allowing the organisation to deliver software safely, predictably, and efficiently.
