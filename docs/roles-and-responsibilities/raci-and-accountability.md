# RACI and Accountability

For complex initiatives, teams should define a **RACI (Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, Involved)** model to clarify ownership across key activities.

## Minimum Expectations

* Critical decisions have a clearly defined accountable role
* Cross-team dependencies are explicitly managed
* Governance and approval points are assigned

## Why It Matters

A RACI model helps teams make accountability visible, reduce ambiguity, and ensure governance obligations are not overlooked. It is particularly valuable where multiple teams, vendors, business units, or approval pathways are involved.

## When to Use It

Teams should strongly consider a RACI model when:
* delivery spans multiple teams or suppliers
* governance approvals involve multiple stakeholders
* accountability is likely to be unclear without explicit mapping
* the initiative is high-risk, high-impact, or operationally complex

## Closing Statement

Clearly defined roles and responsibilities are critical to effective delivery. While team structures may vary, accountability for outcomes must always be explicit, traceable, and aligned with the organisation’s SDLC principles.
