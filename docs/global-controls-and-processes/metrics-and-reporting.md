## Metrics & Reporting
The Metrics & Reporting phase defines how the organisation **measures, monitors, and reports on the performance and health of software delivery**. Metrics provide visibility into delivery progress, quality, operational stability, and compliance, enabling informed decisions at every level—from development teams to leadership.

---

### Objectives
The objectives of Metrics & Reporting are to:

* Provide **transparency** into the software development lifecycle
* Measure delivery effectiveness, efficiency, and quality
* Identify risks, bottlenecks, and improvement opportunities
* Support governance, compliance, and audit requirements
* Enable data-driven decisions for resource allocation, prioritisation, and process improvement

---

### Key Activities
#### Define Key Metrics
* Identify metrics that are meaningful, actionable, and aligned with organisational goals
* Metrics should cover **delivery, quality, operational, and governance dimensions**
* Ensure metrics are relevant for the chosen delivery model (Waterfall, Agile, Product)

**Examples of Metrics by Category:**

| Category                | Example Metrics                                                             |
| ----------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Delivery & Velocity     | Cycle time, lead time, sprint velocity, story throughput                    |
| Quality & Defects       | Defect density, defect resolution time, escaped defects, test coverage      |
| Deployment & Release    | Release frequency, deployment success rate, rollback occurrences            |
| Operations & Stability  | Mean time to recovery (MTTR), availability, incident counts                 |
| Governance & Compliance | Change approval compliance, audit findings, configuration item completeness |

---

#### Data Collection and Tooling
* Collect data automatically where feasible (CI/CD pipelines, monitoring tools, test frameworks)
* Ensure manual data is captured consistently if automation is not possible
* Maintain historical records for trend analysis and reporting

---

#### Reporting and Dashboards
* Produce reports tailored to different stakeholders:

  * **Teams**: sprint progress, backlog health, defect trends
  * **Leadership**: delivery performance, operational health, compliance metrics
  * **Auditors/Regulators**: traceability, approvals, configuration compliance
* Use dashboards for near real-time visibility into key metrics
* Highlight trends, risks, and actionable insights rather than raw numbers

---

#### Review and Feedback
* Conduct regular reviews of metrics with stakeholders
* Identify areas for improvement or process adjustment
* Validate that metrics remain relevant and meaningful
* Avoid metric overload; focus on actionable insights

---

#### Continuous Improvement
* Use metrics to drive retrospectives, lessons learned, and process enhancements
* Track progress on improvement initiatives
* Ensure metrics evolve with organisational goals and delivery practices

---

### Outputs and Deliverables
Typical outputs include:

* Metric definitions, thresholds, and targets
* Dashboards for delivery, quality, and operations
* Periodic reports (weekly, monthly, quarterly)
* Analysis of trends, risks, and improvement opportunities
* Audit-ready records linking metrics to lifecycle activities

---

### Governance and Controls
Key governance requirements:

* **Accuracy and Consistency**: Metrics must be accurate, repeatable, and based on verified data
* **Transparency**: Metrics and dashboards must be accessible to relevant stakeholders
* **Relevance**: Only meaningful and actionable metrics are reported
* **Compliance**: Metrics must support audit and regulatory reporting requirements
* **Review and Accountability**: Metrics are regularly reviewed to ensure organisational alignment

**Control Outcome:**
Metrics provide **clear, actionable insights** into delivery, quality, and governance, enabling informed decision-making and continuous improvement.

---

### Alignment Across Delivery Models
* **Agile / Product Models**:

  * Focus on velocity, cycle time, and release health
  * Metrics support iterative improvements and team-level decision-making

* **Waterfall**:

  * Focus on milestone adherence, defect tracking, and formal audit trails
  * Metrics provide visibility over long-phase delivery and compliance

---

### Proportionality and Risk-Based Metrics
The number and depth of metrics should reflect:

* System criticality and impact
* Regulatory and compliance requirements
* Team size, maturity, and delivery model

Examples:

* High-risk systems require detailed quality, operational, and compliance metrics
* Low-risk internal tools may rely on fewer, high-level indicators

---

### Anti-Patterns to Avoid
* Measuring activity instead of outcomes (e.g., lines of code instead of delivered value)
* Generating metrics that are not actionable
* Ignoring trends and focusing only on point-in-time numbers
* Overloading stakeholders with irrelevant or excessive metrics
* Using metrics to penalise teams rather than improve processes

---

### Closing Statement

Metrics and reporting turn SDLC activities into **evidence-based insights**. They provide visibility, guide improvement, and ensure delivery quality, reliability, and compliance across the organisation.
