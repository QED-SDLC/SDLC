## Plan & Discover
The Plan & Discover phase establishes a clear understanding of the problem to be solved, the value to be delivered, and the most appropriate solution approach. It ensures that delivery efforts are grounded in validated needs, aligned to business objectives, and informed by evidence rather than assumptions.

This phase is critical to reducing delivery risk, avoiding rework, and ensuring that subsequent design and build activities are focused and effective.

---

### Objectives
The objectives of Plan & Discover are to:

* Define the **business problem or opportunity**
* Establish **measurable outcomes and success criteria**
* Understand **user needs and behaviours**
* Explore and evaluate **solution options**
* Identify **risks, constraints, and dependencies**
* Validate assumptions through **research and prototyping**
* Produce a **prioritised and actionable set of requirements**

---

### Key Activities
#### Business and Stakeholder Alignment
* Identify key stakeholders and establish engagement models
* Define business goals, drivers, and strategic alignment
* Agree on scope boundaries and high-level priorities
* Establish success metrics (e.g., KPIs, OKRs)

---

#### User Research and Insights
* Identify target users and personas
* Conduct user research (interviews, observations, surveys)
* Analyse current state processes and pain points
* Validate problems before proposing solutions

---

#### Requirements Definition
* Capture requirements in an appropriate format:

  * User stories and epics (Agile/Product)
  * Business and functional requirements (Waterfall)
* Define acceptance criteria
* Ensure requirements are:

  * Clear, testable, and traceable
  * Prioritised based on value and risk

---

#### Prototyping and Validation (Mandatory for Product Operating Model)
Prototyping is a critical activity used to validate assumptions and refine solutions before full-scale development.

* Develop low- to high-fidelity prototypes as appropriate
* Test prototypes with real users and stakeholders
* Validate usability, feasibility, and desirability
* Iterate based on feedback

**Expectation:**
No significant build activity should commence in a Product Operating Model without evidence of validated assumptions through prototyping or equivalent discovery practices.

---

#### Feasibility and Solution Options
* Explore multiple solution approaches where appropriate
* Assess technical feasibility and constraints
* Consider build vs buy vs reuse options
* Engage architecture and engineering early

---

#### Risk and Dependency Identification
* Identify delivery, technical, security, and operational risks
* Document assumptions and constraints
* Identify dependencies (systems, teams, vendors)
* Define mitigation strategies

---

#### Initial Planning and Estimation
* Develop high-level delivery approach and roadmap
* Estimate effort, cost, and timelines (as appropriate to methodology)
* Define release strategy (incremental vs single release)
* Align with funding and approval processes

---

### Outputs and Deliverables
Outputs should be **fit-for-purpose** based on delivery methodology and risk, but must include:

* Clearly defined **problem statement and objectives**
* **Prioritised backlog** or requirements specification
* **User insights** and research findings
* **Prototypes or validation artefacts** (where applicable)
* Initial **solution options and architectural considerations**
* Documented **risks, assumptions, and dependencies**
* High-level **delivery plan or roadmap**

---

### Governance and Controls
While Plan & Discover encourages exploration and flexibility, the following controls must be met:

* **Traceability**: Requirements must link to business objectives
* **Validation**: Assumptions must be tested where feasible
* **Stakeholder Alignment**: Key stakeholders must agree on scope and objectives
* **Architecture Input**: Solution direction must be reviewed for alignment with standards
* **Risk Visibility**: Key risks must be identified and documented

**Control Outcome:**
Teams must be able to demonstrate that they are solving the *right problem* before progressing to Design and Build.

---

### Tailoring by Delivery Model
#### Agile / Scrum
* Discovery may occur continuously alongside delivery
* Requirements are maintained as a prioritised backlog
* Prototyping and validation occur iteratively

#### Waterfall
* Discovery is typically a defined upfront phase
* Outputs may include formal business cases and requirement specifications
* Greater emphasis on upfront documentation and approvals

#### Product Operating Model
* Discovery is **continuous and embedded** within the product lifecycle
* Strong emphasis on **prototyping, experimentation, and user feedback**
* Decisions are evidence-driven and iteratively refined

---

### Anti-Patterns to Avoid
To maintain effectiveness, teams should avoid:

* Proceeding to build without validated requirements
* Treating assumptions as facts without evidence
* Skipping user engagement or relying solely on internal perspectives
* Over-documenting low-risk initiatives or under-documenting high-risk ones
* Locking in solutions too early without exploring alternatives

---

### Closing Statement

Effective discovery ensures that teams build the right solution, not just deliver a solution right. Investment in this phase reduces downstream risk, improves quality, and increases the likelihood of achieving meaningful business outcomes.
