# Roles and Responsibilities

Clear roles and responsibilities are essential to ensure accountability, effective decision-making, and successful delivery outcomes. While specific role titles and structures may vary across teams and methodologies, the responsibilities defined below must be fulfilled for all initiatives.

Teams may combine roles in smaller initiatives or distribute them across multiple individuals in larger programs; however, accountability for each responsibility must remain aligned and documented.

---

## Core Principles of Accountability

* **Single Point of Accountability**: Each key responsibility must have a clearly identified owner
* **No Gaps or Overlaps**: Responsibilities must be explicitly defined to avoid duplication or omission
* **Methodology-Agnostic**: Responsibilities apply regardless of delivery approach
* **Outcome-Focused**: Accountability is based on outcomes, not just activities or outputs

---

## Shared Responsibilities Across Roles

While roles have defined accountabilities, several responsibilities are shared across the team:

* **Quality**: All roles contribute to delivering a high-quality solution
* **Security**: Security is a shared responsibility embedded in all activities
* **Documentation**: All contributors are responsible for maintaining relevant documentation
* **Risk Management**: Risks must be identified and escalated by all team members
* **Continuous Improvement**: Teams are expected to reflect and improve practices regularly

---

## Role Alignment Across Delivery Models

To support different methodologies:

* In **Agile teams**, responsibilities may be distributed across a cross-functional team
* In **Waterfall delivery**, responsibilities may align to more formal role separation
* In a **Product Operating Model**, roles are typically persistent and aligned to long-lived products

Regardless of structure:

* Responsibilities must be clearly mapped
* Decision-making authority must be understood
* Governance obligations must be met

---

## Closing Statement

Clearly defined roles and responsibilities are critical to effective delivery. While team structures may vary, accountability for outcomes must always be explicit, traceable, and aligned with the organisation’s SDLC principles.
